# HR-Employees-Analysis-Report


## *Reporting Tools:* Excel, Sql, Power BI
*Focus:* Employee Metrics Overview – Performance, Compensation, Demographics, and Departmental Insights  
*Data Summary:* 1,470 employees

## *Introduction/Overview*
This HR dashboard provides a visual summary of key workforce indicators across the organization. It tracks employee count, experience, income, departmental distribution, training, and performance to support effective HR decision-making and resource planning.

## *Key Highlights*
- *Total Employees:* 1,470  
- *Total Companies Worked In:* 3,959 (sum total for all employees)  
- *Monthly Income:* ₦10M (aggregated)  
- *Training Times (Last Year):* 4,115 sessions

## *Workforce Experience & Performance*
- *Total Working Years:*  
  - Majority of employees (~78%) have 17K working years combined  
  - Remaining (~22%) contribute 5K working years
- *Performance Rating Distribution:*  
  - Higher working years correlate with better performance ratings  
  - Indicates that experienced staff are more consistent in delivering value

## *Job Involvement vs Education Field*

- Highest monthly income and job involvement observed in:
- - *Life Sciences* and *Medical* fields  
- *Marketing*, *Technical*, and *HR* departments show lower metrics, indicating possible areas for employee engagement or performance review

## *Compensation Metrics*
- *Hourly Rate & Standard Hours:*  
  - Hourly pay and standard working hours are fairly balanced across employees  
  - Suggests uniform pay structure
- *Daily Rate by Department:*  
  - *Research Department* leads in daily pay (₦2.3M)  
  - Followed by *Sales* (₦0.5M) and *HR* (₦0.2M)

## *Age vs Business Travel*
- Employees who *Travel Frequently* are generally older (avg. age near 40K data point)  
- *Rare Travelers* tend to be younger, possibly newer hires or in less mobile roles

## *Insights & Recommendations*
- *Performance vs Experience:* Utilize senior staff to mentor newer hires for better performance outcomes  
- *Compensation Review:* Investigate pay gaps among departments to ensure equity  
- *Departmental Investment:* HR and Marketing may need more training/resources to boost their output  
- *Talent Retention:* High training frequency suggests a focus on development — monitor retention in relation to training ROI  
- *Job Involvement:* Departments with low involvement could benefit from engagement programs or revised role structures
